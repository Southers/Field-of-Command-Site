MEDITERRANEAN DOCTRINE: MANOEUVRE

CROSSROADS

Compass to Crusader. Long-range logistics and mobile armored warfare across a vast, open theatre.

HISTORICAL CONTEXT:
  • • Desert war: 1,800km from Alexandria to Tripoli
  • • Victory depends on Benghazi and Tobruk supply ports
IN-GAME:
  • → Master long-range supply chains
  • → Balance ambition with fuel limitations
1942
NORTH AFRICA DOCTRINE: COMBINED ARMS

TURNING POINT

Supercharge to Torch. Massed artillery and heavy armor. Large-scale offensives to break the line and destroy the Afrika Korps.

HISTORICAL CONTEXT:
  • • El Alamein: 195,000 Allied vs 104,000 Axis troops
  • • Operation Torch: largest amphibious invasion to date
IN-GAME:
  • → Execute set-piece breaching operations
  • → Coordinate artillery with armor exploitation
1943
ITALY DOCTRINE: DIVISIONAL COMMAND

LIBERATION

Husky to Diadem. First major amphibious landings into Europe. Multi-brigade assaults in difficult mountainous terrain.

HISTORICAL CONTEXT:
  • • Operation Husky: 160,000 troops land in Sicily
  • • Monte Cassino withstands four major assaults
IN-GAME:
  • → Plan amphibious assault operations
  • → Fight mountain warfare and river crossings
1944
NORTH-WEST EUROPE DOCTRINE: VERTICAL ENVELOPMENT

CRUSADE

Overlord to Market Garden. Multi-corps invasion. Coordinate airborne drops with armored thrusts across France.

HISTORICAL CONTEXT:
  • • D-Day: 156,000 troops land on Normandy beaches
  • • Market Garden: 35,000 airborne troops in largest drop
IN-GAME:
  • → Synchronize airborne and seaborne forces
  • → Execute vertical envelopment operations
1945
GERMANY DOCTRINE: TOTAL VICTORY

CAULDRON

Veritable to Plunder. The Rhine Crossing. Complex river operations and the final crushing of resistance in the Reich.

HISTORICAL CONTEXT:
  • • Operation Plunder: 1.3 million troops cross Rhine
  • • 21st Army Group advances 500km in final 60 days
IN-GAME:
  • → Execute opposed river crossing operations
  • → Coordinate coalition forces for final victory
